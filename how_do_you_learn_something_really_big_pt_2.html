How Do You Learn Something Really Big? (Part 2)

After putting together the large framework and some basic rules, I jump to the specifics of the actual time-boxing and goal-setting via 59 Seconds. Here's how it works (again, I modify to suit my needs):

  1. Name your goal. What is it you want to be able to do? This is the same as in Ultralearning (get a job at a top-tier tech company, ace my final exam, win the audition, etc.).
  2. Break the goal down into no more than 5 concrete steps using the following framework:
    • How do you define "done" for this sub-goal?
    • What are the steps you've taken to ensure its success? (Scheduled time in calendar, secured a teacher, etc.)
    • How much time will you give this sub-goal?
    • The final step in the book is to offer yourself a reward, but I've never found this a useful incentive. The goal is its own reward.
  3. I add a retrospective step to gather feedback from outside sources, which could include referencing a lesson in a book to see if I followed a procedure correctly or asking a reliable third party for feedback.
  4. Incorporate feedback into the next sub-goal.

The author also recommends listing the benefits from having achieved the goal and doing what he calls "going public." I've tried both of these steps several times, but I haven't found them effective motivators. I skip them.

Worksheet

  1. Define "done" for your goal
  2. Choose 3-5 sub-goals. For each, do the following:
    1. Define "done" for this sub-goal
    2. List steps you've taken to achieve success (scheduled time, etc.)
    3. How long are you going to work on this sub-goal?
    4. Receive feedback on this sub-goal
    5. Incorporate feedback into next goal
